3. 3D printing & 3D scanning

Project description

  • Choose a 3D printer and determine all the constraints (group project).
  • Design a model and 3D print it.
  • 3D scan an object and document the progress on the website.

3D printer test - group project

The group consisted of me and Katla. We made a 3D printed test to check the tolerance limit of the 3D printer and also determine the design constraints. We used the test recommended by Hafliði, the teacher. The design files for the test were found on Thingiverse. The test was uploaded by the user majda107 and is a mini all in one 3D printer test.

We used Prusa MK3+ and black PLA 3D printing material.

The results showed that the printer passed every test except the overhang part. When the printer reached 60° overhang it began to fail.

Design and 3D printing

Preparation

I wanted to make an object that could be useful. I googled 3D printing ideas and found a Shower Cubicle two sided hook on Thingiverse..

The hook was made by the user kimmy_p. I liked the idea and wanted to make something similar. I made a sketch of the idea and all the dimensions.

The next step was to begin drawing in Fusion 360.

Drawing in Fusion 360

I began drawing in Fusion 360. The pictures below show the process.

The bridge test on the mini all in one 3D test showed that the 3D printer could handle the bridge on my hook. I exported the design from Fusion 360 as a STL file and imported it to Prusa Slicer, an open-source slicer software developed for Prusa 3D printers.

Prusa Slicer and 3D printing

The picutres below show how the 3D printer printed my object. The infill setting was 15%.

I knew the bridge on the hook could be a problem but luckily the Prusa Slicer sliced the model the correct way as can be seen on the lower picture to the left. It took the printer around 3.5 hours to print the model.

The final results look exactly like I had imagined.

3D Scanning

This last part of Assignment 3 was to 3D scan an object. I used Qlone, an all-in-one 3D scanner app. I downloaded the app on my phone and printed the mat Qlone recommends. I watched two Qlone 3D scanning tutorials to get to know the app (links below).

I decided to 3D scan a toothpaste tube.

It was nice to use Qlone because it told me exactly what to do.

I made sure I was 3D scanning in a well-lit room. However the results were not as good as I had hoped for, I tried it several times and the best outcome is in a gif here below.
